MyPath(s)

When your plan isn't going the way your way

Problem Description

Overview

When I got home, I went around the entire house and I talked to many of my friends. I asked them two questions:

  • What are small personal problems that bother you that you wish could be fixed through a web application?
  • What are some problems you have faced since you have arrived at the University of Washington that you wish they did better?
I got a lot of answers regarding the personal problems, but many of ideas were not feasible for a web application. Instead, a common theme that showed up was whether or not I could make something that could guide them through many different majors because many of them are starting their junior year without a major and that can be tough to go through mentally. The problem is that most of the majors at UW are very competitive, so it's hard for new students to get into their dream major if they were not directly admitted into it. Thus, while they chase their dream major, they never come up with a backup plan incase they don't get into their major and this can be a real problem when they are at face with a rejection letter. This is the reality of many students on campus, and it was once a reality of mine until I got into Informatics on my second try!

Information Technology Problem

One of the problems with information technology in this space is that students are not well informed of the many major options they have. Yes, a student can go to an advisor, but what if they are booked and they simply don't have time to go see one? There needs to be more options as to how they get retrieve more information quickly to help them guide down a path or two. Students are also not well informed about how the classes they have already taken can actually be applied to multiple different majors other than their dream major. Lastly, many students do not think of creating a backup plan. I know I didn't, and I was almost too late when I realized. Every student can achieve success, they just need a little guidance when doing so.

Existing Solutions

One of the best existing solutions for students to use that enables them to plan their classes is UW's MyPlan. This app enables to you pick what major you want to pursue and shows you all of the prerequisites and requirements to graduate with that degree. Along with that, it also shows what courses you have completed that fit under that degree and the ones that don't. This is a handy tool if you know exactly what you are doing because it can help guide you to that end goal.

Proposed Application

Application Description

MyPath(s) would be an interactive tool that students may use when they are in need of quick and easy guidance when it comes to planning the rest of their time at the university. Students would be able to use MyPath(s) to input what classes they have taken at UW and the app will create a list of majors that those classes are a prerequisite to as well what other classes they must complete to apply to that major. This will also enable them to see what other majors are similar to the main one they are interested in, ones they may have never heard of. This application is not to be used to go down one path, but instead give you the ability to choose what path you want to take out of the many and have a backup plan just incase.

Application Users

The users for this application would be the many students at the University of Washington's campus as this would be hard to intergrate into other schools since course names and numbers are different everywhere and majors may vary. This application will mainly focus on the freshmen and sophomores on campus, as well as all of the students that are not in their major yet.

Interacting with MyPath(s)

  • Users will first choose 2-3 different main majors that they are interested in
  • Then they will input the classes they have completed manually by typing them in, or they can choose from a list of courses that are sorted by subject.
  • MyPath(s) will take the related courses they have taken and the majors they selected as their favorites and then display a list of majors and their descriptions. It will also display the kind of jobs that can be obtained with that certain degree.
  • The user will then click on a major that peaks their interest and then it will display the other classes they have to complete to apply to this major, as well as the courses they have already taken that apply to it.
  • They can then go back and look at other majors that they may be interested in.

Solution to the Problem

This is a solution to the problem that I described at the top of the page because many students are lost and they have no idea where to look first. Having something that is so easy and accessible will make their time at UW a lot smoother, and it will enable them to walk around with more confidence because it will feel as if they are finally doing something right. This will help students create backup plans out of the classes they have already taken, so that they aren't wasting any time because time here is so valuable. Using this application will allow them to explore majors that they may have never heard of and it may be exactly what they were looking for! This is the complete opposite of MyPlan because when you are using MyPlan, you already know what major you want to do and you can only see if your classes fit for that particular major. You are unable to see what other majors you are able to apply for with the classes you have taken without auditing another major one by one.
